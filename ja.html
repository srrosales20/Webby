User Experience and Accessibility


Impactful Design

  • Creating accessible web pages keeping all possible needs in mind means that you will create accessible web pages for everyone
  • This means that taking that leading with emapthy when considering your audience or consumer can go a long way in the success and user interface with your web page
  • Many features that were inteded to increase access for disabled people have become useful and in some cases essential for able-bodied people as well. This shows us how a focus on accessibility can actually promote innovation.

The Law

Your web page is a considered a place of common accomodation and must follow the rules and regulations created by WCAG 2.1

The Law States that:

  • Your page must be perceivable

    • This means that your webpage must have multiple forms of being perceived. For example, including text that can be visually read, or read out loud using a text-to-speech feature.

  • Your page musr be operable

    • This refers to enabling features which allow for your content to be accessed in various formats. For example, allowing someone with mobility limitations a feature to control their screen using remote commands.

  • Your page must be clear

    • This point is staightforward: your website should have a clear purpose and comprehensible content

  • Your page must be robust

    • This refers to filling your web page with sufficient content to fully understand your message and purpose. Put yourself in the shoes of your client and ask "Is the purpose of the webpage clear and direct?"
